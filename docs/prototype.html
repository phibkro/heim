About v1.0 · updated 2025

I started programming because I wanted to understand how things actually work — not just how to use them. That pull toward first principles still drives everything I build.

My work sits at the intersection of web development, functional programming, and software architecture. I care about systems that are correct by design, not by luck or test coverage alone.

Lately I've been deep in AI agent development — building tools that handle repetitive engineering work while keeping developers in control of the decisions that actually matter.

I'm self-taught, currently finishing a Bachelor's in Informatics at NTNU. I learn best by building things that stretch what I know and then figuring out why they break.
